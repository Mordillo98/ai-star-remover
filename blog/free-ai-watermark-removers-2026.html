1. Exact reversal (this site)

The Gemini sparkle is an alpha blend with a fixed, published map — so you compute the original, not guess it. Most precise for this specific mark, and in-browser so nothing uploads. Trade-off: only targets the Gemini sparkle in its known position; a moved or distorted mark won't match. Try it →

2. Cropping the corner

The laziest solution that actually works: the star sits bottom-right, so crop a few percent. No tool, perfect privacy, zero artifacts in what remains — but you lose area.

3. Photoshop / Photopea (clone stamp)

Clone-stamping works on any watermark and stays local. Manual and slow per image; result depends on your hand. Photopea is a capable free, in-browser alternative.

4. Generic AI “object eraser” webs

One-click, often free to a limit. The catch: many upload your image to process server-side. Read the privacy policy before pasting anything sensitive. Client-side is the test for private use.

What to avoid

  • Tools claiming to remove SynthID — it's designed to resist removal; promises are ineffective or worse.
  • Installer-bundled “free” downloaders — for a corner watermark you need no desktop software.
  • Using removal to hide AI provenance where disclosure is required — see the law page.

How to choose

  • Precision + privacy, Gemini mark: this tool.
  • Any watermark, don't mind manual work: clone stamp.
  • Can lose the corner: just crop.
  • Not the Gemini mark, want one click: a client-side AI eraser (check its policy).
